# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is the private harness owner working inside CommonPlace. They need to understand how their harness is growing, which skills it has proven, when it is ready to claim a level, and how that history connects to signed lineage. Public viewers are secondary and see only fields the owner explicitly publishes.

## Product Purpose

CommonPlace is the product home for the Growth Layer. It turns Theorem's posterior, evidence, identity, and graph-version mechanics into an understandable owner-facing surface without changing those mechanics or duplicating their source of truth. Success means the owner can inspect a signed card, follow historical growth, understand the live mathematics, inspect note Stamps, and compare read-only marketplace listings using live, verifiable data.

## Brand Personality

Precise, companionable, and quietly distinctive. The surrounding product should feel calm and trustworthy. The collectible card may feel special and crafted because it is a signed projection of real history.

## Anti-references

- A gamified dashboard whose points are detached from evidence.
- A crypto marketplace with speculative transaction chrome.
- A generic uniform card grid that hides the importance of different Growth objects.
- A canvas-only visualization that excludes keyboard and assistive-technology users.
- A legacy harness-console skin nested inside the CommonPlace shell.

## Design Principles

1. Mechanics before metaphor: every visible stat resolves to Theorem evidence or signed lineage.
2. One product language: CommonPlace owns the shell, navigation, controls, and states.
3. Earned distinction: the signed collectible card is exceptional; ordinary interface chrome remains restrained.
4. Private by default: publication is explicit and field-specific.
5. Honest absence: unavailable live data is named, never replaced by a reachable fixture.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA for contrast and interaction. All controls must be keyboard reachable with visible focus. Tabs follow the ARIA Authoring Practices pattern. The Mathematics view has a semantic DOM equivalent and a static fallback. Reduced motion removes spatial and ambient movement while preserving state feedback.
